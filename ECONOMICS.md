# Economics

## Overview

StackWise is designed as a lead-generation product for Credex by helping startups identify unnecessary AI tooling costs and optimization opportunities.

The core business model assumption is that a percentage of users discovering meaningful savings opportunities would eventually purchase discounted AI infrastructure credits through Credex.

---

# Estimated Lead Value

If a startup spends:
- $500–$3000/month on AI tooling
- and Credex helps reduce those costs through discounted infrastructure credits,

then a converted customer could reasonably generate hundreds or thousands of dollars in annual transaction value.

For estimation purposes:
- Average converted lead value: ~$1000–$5000 annually

This is based on recurring AI infrastructure spending behavior among engineering teams.

---

# Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

Potential zero-budget acquisition channels:
- Product Hunt launch
- Hacker News
- Founder Twitter/X communities
- Reddit communities related to AI tooling
- Engineering newsletters
- LinkedIn founder content

Estimated early-stage CAC:
- Organic founder communities: low CAC
- Paid ads: likely expensive initially

The tool is designed to work best as an organic viral acquisition loop.

---

# Conversion Funnel Assumptions

Example funnel:

- 1000 landing page visitors
- 300 completed audits
- 60 lead captures
- 10 consultation calls
- 2 paying infrastructure customers

This would create:
- 20% lead conversion from audits
- 16% consultation conversion
- 20% close rate from calls

---

# Path to $1M ARR

To reach $1M ARR:
- Credex would need recurring infrastructure customers
- Strong viral sharing from audit reports
- Consistent founder distribution channels
- High-value startup accounts with large AI spend

The audit tool itself acts as:
- lead generation
- trust building
- inbound acquisition infrastructure

rather than direct monetization.

---

# Important Assumptions

This MVP uses simplified assumptions because:
- real enterprise AI spend varies significantly
- infrastructure negotiations are highly custom
- startup AI usage patterns evolve quickly

Future versions could:
- benchmark spend by company size
- estimate infrastructure migration savings
- include enterprise pricing intelligence
