# User Interviews

## Interview 1

### Person
Adarsh Tyagi — Visual Designer at Blinkit

### Company Stage
Large-scale product company

### Key Quotes
- “Different teams often subscribe to AI tools independently without much visibility.”
- “A clean and trustworthy UI matters a lot for financial recommendation tools.”
- “The savings summary should feel simple and immediately understandable.”

### Most Surprising Insight
The user emphasized trust, clarity, and presentation quality more than raw feature depth.

### What It Changed
I focused more on improving the SaaS-style UI, recommendation clarity, and overall dashboard presentation.

## Interview 2

### Person
Abhinav — Computer Science Student

### Company Stage
Student / Personal project usage

### Key Quotes
- “I use ChatGPT almost daily but rarely track how much these tools cost over time.”
- “Many students and developers subscribe impulsively and forget recurring payments.”
- “A tool showing total yearly spending would be useful.”

### Most Surprising Insight
The user was more surprised by annualized costs rather than monthly subscriptions.

### What It Changed
I made annual savings more visually prominent in the audit results page.

## Interview 3

### Person
Piyush — Computer Science Student

### Company Stage
Student / Side-project experimentation

### Key Quotes
- “People use multiple AI tools together even when they overlap heavily.”
- “Recommendations should explain WHY a tool overlap exists.”
- “I’d trust the audit more if the reasoning looked practical and realistic.”

### Most Surprising Insight
The user cared more about recommendation reasoning quality than aggressive savings estimates.

### What It Changed
I improved recommendation messaging to feel more practical and less overly generic.
